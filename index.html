Greenhouse Gas Emissions
Over a Decade (2010-2020)

Greenhouse gases are gases that absorb infrared radiation such as carbon dioxide and methane. When emitted into our atmosphere, these gases trap the sun's heat, leading to global warming and climate change.



What countries have the highest emissions?

China, the United States, India, Russia, and Japan were the 5 countries with the highest greenhouse gas emissions in the world in the decade 2010-2020.

What about the rest of the world?

The combined greehouse gas emissions in the 5 countries of China, the United States, India, Russia, and Japan have made up 48-52% of all global greenhouse gas emissions throughout the decade of 2010-2020, with the top country of China making up for 22-27% of global emissions alone.


Breakdown of emissions in the United States.

From 2010 to 2020, the Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program (GHGRP) collected greenhouse gas emissions data from the largest emitting facilities in the U.S. While this data does not include emissions from every source, it shows which states are the largest contributors.

How have emissions changed over time in the United States?

The United States has made some consistent progress in reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Among the most abundant greenhouse gases in the U.S. throughout the decade were carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide, and fluorinated gases.





Why Should We Care About Greenhouse Gases?


Greenhouse gases are the primary driver of climate change. Climate change is detrimental to our world as we know it because it leads to a rise in earth's temperature, an increase in severe weather events, rising sea levels, and much more. These changes can have major implications for life on Earth, such as human health and agricultural productivity. If we don't continue to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, the impacts of climate change will be felt worldwide.
